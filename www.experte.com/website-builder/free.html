In the past, websites were exclusive. Setting one up required either detailed programming knowledge or lots of money to pay a developer to do it for you. Thanks to website-building software, anyone can create a digital presence. Some site builders even offer free (limited) versions of their platforms.

We reviewed 11 website builders, comparing their user-friendliness, design tools, and features. Should you want unlimited access to everything they have to offer, you'll need to reach for your wallet and purchase a monthly subscription. In most cases, these include hosting and domain costs, making website builders a practical 'one-stop-shop' for anyone looking to digitally get things done.

With that said, some providers market free, limited versions of their platforms. For example, you might be able to build a site at no cost, but have to pay for hosting. In other cases, both the builder and hosting (via the provider's subdomains) are free, but you'll need to pay for storage space or tolerate advertising banners on your site.

Should you be able to make do with these limitations, or just want to try it before you buy it, we'll show you what free site-building options are out there:

How Can I Make Create a Website for Free?

Creating a free website varies from platform to platform. With that said, the steps below are common to almost any site builder:

  1. 1.
    Create an account: In order to build a website, you'll need an account with the provider you've selected. Among services that offer free versions, this shouldn't take long as you won't need to input any payment information.
  2. 2.
    Select a template: Site builders work their magic by using design templates with ready-made layouts. Free versions often offer only a limited selection of these.
  3. 3.
    Edit the template: Once you've settled on a design template, you'll be able to adjust it to your preferences in the editor. Things like colors, fonts, backgrounds, or other aspects can always be modified.
  4. 4.
    Add menus and pages: Websites generally consist of multiple pages and sub-pages. When using a site builder, these are easy to add and integrate into your website's navigation menu.
  5. 5.
    Add text, images, and other content: By default, design templates are filled with placeholder content (usually text). When adding your texts, images, or videos to your website, you'll replace this. Of course, you can then edit your content's appearance in the site builder's editor or add new elements (such as forms, testimonials, or blogs). If using a platform's free version, chances are that you'll only be given limited access to these features.
  6. 6.
    Publish your website: Once you're satisfied with your website's appearance, all that's left to do is publish it. For this, you can select a premium domain, or host it for free using your site builder's domain.

How this entire process actually goes depends on the site builder you've selected. If you'd like to see how things went with EXPERTE.com's top performance, Wix, you can check out our step-by-step guide.

Disadvantages of Free Websites

As we mentioned above, free versions of premium site builders are only suitable for personal or small projects. They have a few major disadvantages, which we've summarized below for you:

  • Unprofessional subdomains
    A domain like https://username.wixsite.com/webaddress isn't nice to look at. They look cheap and amateurish, giving off the impression that the owner wasn't willing to invest in a unique domain. Especially for businesses, free websites aren't a viable, long-term option.

  • SEO disadvantages
    Most site builders allow their paying customers to optimize the way that search engines find their websites (SEO). This isn't the case for free versions, and again, the lack of a 'proper' domain makes this even worse. How often have you seen a website with a site builder's subdomain on the first or even tenth page of Google results? If it's challenging for search engines to find your website, it will be even more difficult for visitors and potential customers.

  • Limited features
    Creativity usually suffers most on free site builders, since the number of available features is limited. Caps on storage space and bandwidth artificially suppress your site's growth as well.

The good news: You can upgrade from a free version to a premium one with a unique domain very easily.

And What About Free Online Stores?

As you can see, free site builders are really only suitable for smaller or personal websites. If you'd like to sell products and accept payments, your choices are reduced to a handful. We're only aware of a few ecommerce platforms that allow non-paying users to make sales and/or accept payments.

Of these, Ecwid is the best choice: With its free version, you can up to 10 physical products. You won't be able to offer coupons, use inventory management, take advantage of automatic tax calculations, or access advanced SEO tools, however. One nifty feature is that your free Ecwid shop can easily be integrated into another site builder.

Conclusion

Do you want a free website? You can easily have one but will need to accept a few limitations: Ugly provider subdomains and branding, as well as storage and bandwidth caps, which will hamper its growth sooner or later.

For basic projects, personal blogs, or trying out a platform before purchasing a subscription, you'll do well with the free services we've described above. And in case you do want to make the figurative jump from free user to paying subscriber, upgrading is easy on any of the platforms we've looked at.
