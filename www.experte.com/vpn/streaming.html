Netflix was just the beginning; video streaming is growing in popularity, and the number of services offering it seems to increase from year to year. To keep up with all the series and movies that everyone is talking about, as well as pop culture, you'll need more and more accounts.

However, because streaming services' content libraries vary from country to country, this can be complicated. For example, a movie that's available on Netflix in the US might only be available on Acorn TV in the UK or limited to a local media library. You can forget about these regional differences if using a VPN, since they make it possible to access otherwise geo-blocked content from anywhere.

Why Stream With a VPN?

Streaming services like Netflix, Disney+, and others don't acquire global rights for movies and series. Rather, licensing agreements are made country by country, explaining why the content that is available varies based on the viewers' location. So, for example, if you're reading about an interesting Netflix series on an American site, you may not find it in the UK's Netflix catalog. Connecting with a VPN changes that, making it possible to watch that show or movie as if you were in the US.

A VPN doesn't just mask your identity on the internet; thanks to its globally-distributed servers, it also provides users with an IP address from a country of their choosing. Therefore, if you (physically in the UK) connect to a VPN server in the US and then access Netflix, the US catalog will be displayed.

However, not every VPN service is suitable for streaming. The truth is, using a VPN to stream otherwise geo-blocked content from abroad is something of a legal gray area (more on this later), and streaming services do exert some effort in preventing users from bypassing region restrictions.

We assessed 27 VPN providers, comparing how they fared in terms of accessing streaming content from abroad. Due to the massive list of available streaming services, we limited our review to Netflix, Amazon Prime Video, Disney+, and the BBC's iPlayer.

How Does Streaming via VPN Work?

Once you've chosen a VPN provider, using it to access streaming content from abroad is a breeze.

1. Install the VPN's client or app on your device and launch the program.

2. From the server list, select the country in which you want to access streaming content and connect to a server there.

Select a server in the country which you want to stream content from.

3. Once connected, visit the streaming service of your choice. If you were already on the website before you established the VPN connection, you'll need to reload the page.

With a VPN, you can access series or movies that aren't available in your location's Netflix catalog.

4. You can now browse the selected country's streaming catalog.

Performance

While video streaming isn't very demanding, it still requires a stable Internet connection. Poor quality or constant buffering can instantly spoil any viewing experience. You can find out which VPN services offer the fastest and most reliable connections in EXPERTE.com's VPN speed ranking.

Server Infrastructure

In order to watch movies and series from another country, you'll need to log in to them with an IP address from that country. Therefore, it's important that your chosen VPN has streaming servers in the country where your favorite content is located. Most VPNs have good coverage of Europe, North America, and parts of Asia.

Is It Possible to Stream With Free VPNs?

No. We aren't aware of any free VPNs that allow users to reliably access content from abroad on major platforms. Even "freemium" VPN services, which are streaming-capable, usually don't offer this in their free versions.

Of course, on occasion, it may be possible to access some streaming services with a free VPN, however, reliable video streaming via VPN requires a paid subscription.

Is It Legal to Stream With a VPN?

Streaming video content with a VPN is not illegal. Even though you're not breaking any laws, the truth is, that you're operating in a bit of a gray area. This is because by circumventing region restrictions, you're violating the streaming platform's terms of service. As a result, your account could be blocked if they discover you're using a VPN, however, this is unlikely and we aren't aware of any related account closures or suspensions.

In short, you don't have to fear any legal consequences when streaming via VPN.

Conclusion

VPNs help get the most out of any streaming services that you already pay for. Netflix and others offer different movies and series from country to country. Using a VPN to change your virtual location makes it possible to access a huge selection of new content with the tap or click of a button. While you'll likely be violating the platform's terms of service, you don't have to fear any legal consequences.

That being said, not all VPNs are streaming-capable, and not all streaming-capable VPNs manage to bypass every services' region restrictions. Our Top 5 VPN services made content from abroad available on some of the major streaming services. Just remember to find out whether your favorite service is currently supported before signing a contract.
