Best HideMyAss Alternatives: Which VPN Is Faster?

There aren't many alternatives to a VPN if you're interested in online anonymity and streaming otherwise geo-restricted content. HideMyAss (HMA) is one of the most well-known VPNs available, however, not necessarily the best as quite a few other services excel in different areas.

In our EXPERTE.com VPN comparison series, we carefully assessed 27 providers. Below, we'll let you know the best alternatives to HMA and their strengths and weaknesses.

Potential Disadvantages of HideMyAss

You might be wondering why you need an alternative to HideMyAss in the first place. The VPN doesn't do anything wrong per se, as our review showed, however, it does have some weaknesses that its competitors don't, such as:

  • Outdated app
    The app's mascot (a colorful and cheeky donkey) certainly has fans among the userbase, however, we're not among them. To us, the app shows its age and we'd prefer something more modern and elegant, like those offered by its competitors.

  • Mixed streaming performance
    During our test, we could only occasionally rely on HMA to grant us access to otherwise unavailable streaming content. We couldn't watch Netflix and Amazon Prime Video content from abroad, despite its dedicated streaming servers. Luckily, we didn't encounter any issues with Disney+ and BBC iPlayer. Under ideal conditions, a VPN should grant access to all streaming catalogs.

  • No split tunneling (desktop)
    Split tunneling is a useful VPN feature: It allows you to specify which apps or websites use your VPN or regular connection. HMA offers this, but only for Android devices, and not in the desktop app.

  • Located in the UK
    When it comes to where a VPN is based, there are several different views: Some users prefer offshore VPNs located in places like Panama or the British Virgin Islands, since they adhere to less restrictive data privacy laws and aren't members of intelligence-sharing agreements. Others see countries with strict data privacy laws as better since it means that information is in safer hands.

    HMA is based in the UK and belongs to the second group. Whether that's an advantage or disadvantage depends on you.

  • Doesn't offer SmartDNS
    Many VPN services include Smart DNS, a feature that makes it possible to bypass country restrictions on just about any Internet-capable device. HideMyAss is not among them.

  • No WireGuard
    WireGuard is widely seen as the new gold standard among VPN protocols. Many of the biggest VPN services have integrated it, however, once again, HMA isn't among them.

Our price-performance winner, Surfshark, has only been on the market since 2017, however, it's quickly and rightfully established itself as one of the leading VPNs. Based in the British Virgin Islands, the VPN offers apps for Windows, macOS, Linux, Android, and iOS, as well as Android TV, Apple TV, and Fire TV.

What we like

Surfshark's relatively low cost is definitely a big advantage, however, that's far from the only thing we liked about the VPN: The program scores owing to its top security standards, powerful performance, and a wide variety of features. Surfshark was EXPERTE.com's top price-performance pick.

Are you looking for a streaming VPN? If so, ExpressVPN is worth checking out.

Proton VPN is a Swiss open-source VPN that has continually improved over the past few years and is now just a step or two away from our winner's podium. It offers apps for Windows, macOS, Linux, iOS, and Android, as well as Chromebooks and Android TVs. If desired, it can also be manually installed on routers or other hardware.

What we like

There are two things in particular that we like about Proton VPN:

  1. 1.
    Its emphasis on user privacy and security is visible in its open-source approach and range of features. For example, "Secure Core" makes it possible to route traffic through an additional VPN server in a country with strict data privacy laws, before heading to your target country.
  2. 2.
    The service also scores by offering the most generous free VPN package on the market, setting no data limits whatsoever. This means that you can surf as much as you'd like, even though you're limited to a small number of server locations and cannot stream video content.

In our opinion, Proton VPN offers the best freemium VPN on the market.

What makes Proton VPN better than HideMyAss?

Proton VPN is open source, whereas HMA isn't. Alongside useful security features that HMA doesn't offer, Proton VPN also includes split tunneling. During our speed test, Proton VPN finished a few places ahead of HMA, staking out second place overall. Rounding things out, for streaming, the Swiss provider also did well, allowing us to access otherwise unavailable content catalogs on all four providers we tested.

Private Internet Access (PIA), from the US, belongs to the same (somewhat dubious) parent company as ExpressVPN. Its apps are offered for Windows, macOS, Linux, Android, and iOS, with browser extensions for Chrome, Firefox, and Opera.

What we like

PIA's unique app is the reason it features on this list: Its modules can be customized to your individual preferences. With other types of software, this might seem unremarkable, however, it's a true rarity in the world of VPNs. And we couldn't be happier.

The Quick Connect module displays your favorites and recently used servers, while Quick Settings is where you can turn things like port forwarding on or off. In other words, you can choose which features and information should be displayed in the VPN's app.

Beyond that, PIA scores with its large server network and solid streaming performance (we only had trouble with Disney+).

PIA's VPN app can be fully customized to your preferences and needs.

What makes Private Internet Access better than HideMyAss?

PIA offers a much better app and was more reliable in terms of streaming than HMA. Its selection of features is also wider: Split tunneling is available for desktop users, as is Smart DNS. Rounding out the positives, the WireGuard protocol is integrated.

Conclusion

HideMyAss is a decent VPN, however, it suffers from a few shortcomings. Thankfully, there are a number of good alternatives that are a step ahead of HMA:

NordVPN offers what is likely the best overall VPN package on the market, Surfshark excels in terms of its price-performance ratio, ExpressVPN is a user-friendly, streaming-focused all-arounder, ProtonVPN is all about security and open source, and Private Internet Access serves up a fully customizable app.

Other alternatives to HideMyAss can be found in our comprehensive EXPERTE.com VPN comparison series.
